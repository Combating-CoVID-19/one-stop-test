Outdoors

The most important place to stay safe
For what can i go outside?
    Regulations vary from state to state, but a widespread and accepted norm is that you can go outside as long as you continue to practice social distancing, keep at least six feet of distance between each individual you meet, and not attend group activities or gatherings.
What can I do outside?
    You can bike, walk, jog, and run by yourself. You can also hike and enjoy nature. You can birdwatch, photograph wildlife, and record plants and animals you see. Make sure you follow safe practices and don’t affect the local ecosystem. Make sure you do not litter.

Cautions
  • Do not touch playground equipment, as they may be contaminated,
  • Do not sit on benches, as they may be contaminated.
  • Do not use picnic tables, as they are not sanitized and may be contaminated.
  • Do not go swimming, the water may be contaminated.
  • Do not go rock climbing, the water may be contaminated.
  • Do not touch any surfaces in general or come into contact with items that people are likely to touch such as fences.
    CDC: Here

Tips
  • If you encounter someone walking towards you from down the sidewalk, step away onto the grass or the side of the road if the street you are walking by has an emergency lane. Do not step into the path of automobiles and other vehicles!
  • Use 70% isopropyl alcohol to clean your glasses, wash down your bike, and wash your clothes/jacket before using them again.
  • Wash your hands, feet, face, and neck when you get home (before you touch anything). Maybe even shower.

Can I go Shopping?
    Shopping for essential goods is usually allowed in most states. Just be wary of all situations and practice social distancing. Wear a mask, order online when possible, sanitize your products when you get back to your residence, don’t use public or group transportation services whenever possible, only enter the store if it is sparsely populated, wash your car, and wash your hands.
